Algollusion

Field: Computational Economics / Industrial Organization | JEL Codes: C63, D43, D83, L13, L41

Definition

Autonomous emergence of collusive pricing behavior among algorithmic agents through reinforcement learning without explicit communication.

Etymology

Portmanteau combining "algorithm" + "collusion". The construction elegantly preserves the morphological clarity of both root concepts while creating a memorable 4-syllable term. The blended form follows successful economics neologism patterns (cf. "tokenomics" = token + economics). The double-L at the junction maintains pronounceability while signaling the collusion component.

Usage Example

Recent evidence suggests that algollusion emerges even when firms lack explicit coordination mechanisms, as reinforcement learning algorithms independently converge on collusive equilibria through repeated interaction and mutual observation of pricing signals.
